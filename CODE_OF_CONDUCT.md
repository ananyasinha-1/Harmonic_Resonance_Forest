# Code of Conduct

### Our Commitment

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone participating in the Harmonic Resonance Forest (HRF) project, regardless of:

- Age
- Experience level
- Nationality
- Educational background
- Gender identity and expression
- Personal appearance
- Race or ethnicity
- Religion
- Disability status

Our goal is to promote scientific collaboration, innovation, and respectful discussion while maintaining the integrity of research contributions.

---

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and professional in all interactions.
- Provide constructive feedback and criticism.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Focus discussions on technical, scientific, and project-related matters.
- Support new contributors and learners.
- Maintain honesty in research, benchmarking, and reporting.
- Follow project guidelines and contribution standards.
- Credit original work and cite sources where appropriate.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
- Personal attacks, insults, or inflammatory comments.
- Deliberate misinformation or manipulation of research results.
- Submission of plagiarized code, documentation, or research.
- Publishing confidential or private information without permission.
- Trolling, spamming, or disruptive behavior.
- Artificially inflating benchmark results or misrepresenting experimental outcomes.
- Any conduct that could damage the credibility of the project or its community.

---

## Research Integrity

As HRF is a research-focused project, contributors must:

- Report results honestly and transparently.
- Avoid selective reporting of experimental outcomes.
- Clearly document methodology and assumptions.
- Reproduce experiments whenever possible.
- Maintain scientific rigor in pull requests and discussions.
- Respect peer review and technical feedback.

Any attempt to falsify data, manipulate benchmarks, or misrepresent performance may result in immediate removal from the project.

---

## Contribution Guidelines

Before submitting contributions:

1. Read the project's documentation.
2. Follow coding standards and repository structure.
3. Ensure code is tested and documented.
4. Provide clear descriptions in pull requests.
5. Maintain reproducibility for research-related changes.
6. Be open to review comments and requested improvements.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying acceptable behavior.
- Reviewing reports fairly and objectively.
- Taking appropriate corrective action when necessary.
- Protecting contributors from harassment and misconduct.

Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers through:

- GitHub Issues (for project-related concerns)
- GitHub Discussions
- Direct communication with repository maintainers

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially whenever possible.

---

## Enforcement Actions

Depending on the severity of the violation, maintainers may:

1. Issue a warning.
2. Request corrective action.
3. Temporarily restrict participation.
4. Revoke contribution privileges.
5. Permanently ban individuals from project spaces.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by the principles of the Contributor Covenant and adapted for research-oriented open-source collaboration.

---

The HRF (Harmonic Resonance Forest) project thrives through respectful communication, scientific integrity, and meaningful contributions from the global open-source community.
